Maryland

Restaurant

Thames Street Oyster House
Location: 1728 Thames St, Baltimore, MD 21231
Contact Info: (443) 449-7726
Website: https://www.thamesstreetoysterhouse.com
About: This casual seafood place has a full bar and outdoor seating, and it’s great for group events. The food is well worth the price, and the service is great; you should definitely head here before you leave Maryland.

Living Quarters

Hotel Indigo Baltimore Downtown
Location: 24 West Franklin St, Baltimore, MD 21201
Contact Info: (410) 625-6200
Website: http://www.baltimoreindigohotel.com
About: As a moderately priced hotel with beautiful decor and free Wifi, this hotel is definitely a place to consider when looking for a place to stay in Maryland. It also has a wonderful restaurant within it with great food, and it’s in great proximity to other businesses in the city.

Landmark

Fort McHenry National Monument
Location: 2400 E Fort Ave, Baltimore, MD 21230
Contact Info: (410) 962-4290
Website: http://www.nps.gov
About: This National Monument is a great place to visit to see some of America’s history. Take a tour and see where Francis Scott Key was inspired to write the National Anthem, as well as a fort used by the US army in both World Wars. It’s also kid-friendly!

Spa

About Faces Day Spa & Salon
Location: 1501 S Clinton St, Fl 3, Baltimore, MD 21224
Contact Info: (410) 675-0099
Website: https://www.aboutfacesdayspa.com
About: The About Faces Day Spa & Salon offers hair care services as well as massages, facials, nail care, makeup services, and more. You’ll get a lot for your money if you come here before you leave the state.

Museum

American Visionary Art Museum
Location: 800 Key Hwy, Baltimore, MD 21230
Contact Info: (410) 244-1900
Website: http://www.avam.org
About: This eclectic museum is an amalgamation of art pieces from people of all different backgrounds, from farmers and housewives to the disabled and homeless, and even some neurosurgeons. It’s a great place to come to see a wide selection of atypical artworks depicting the hopes and heartbreaks of those you wouldn’t typically see as artists.
